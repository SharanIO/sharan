My Journey in Robotics

My fascination with robotics began in my childhood, where I spent countless hours building and tinkering with mechanical toys. This early curiosity evolved into a passionate pursuit of understanding how machines could be made to interact intelligently with their environment. Growing up in India, I was surrounded by a rich culture of innovation and problem-solving, which deeply influenced my approach to engineering.

During my undergraduate years at SRM Institute of Science and Technology, I discovered my true calling in robotics engineering. The combination of mechanical engineering, computer science, and electronics opened up a world of possibilities. I led the university's robotics team, where we developed autonomous systems for various competitions, teaching me not just technical skills but also the importance of collaboration and leadership.

My journey took an exciting turn when I moved to the United States to pursue my Master's in Robotics at the University at Buffalo. Here, I've had the opportunity to work on cutting-edge projects in soft robotics, computer vision, and human-robot interaction. Each project has been a stepping stone, teaching me new approaches to solving complex engineering challenges while maintaining a focus on practical, real-world applications.

What drives me is the potential of robotics to transform lives and industries. Whether it's developing more intuitive human-robot interfaces or creating robots that can safely navigate complex environments, I'm passionate about pushing the boundaries of what's possible. I believe in creating solutions that are not just technically sophisticated but also accessible and beneficial to society.

Outside of my technical work, I'm an advocate for STEM education and regularly mentor aspiring roboticists. I believe in sharing knowledge and inspiring the next generation of engineers. When I'm not working with robots, you might find me exploring photography, practicing guitar, or hiking - activities that help me maintain a creative and balanced perspective in my engineering work.

"In robotics, every challenge is an opportunity to innovate, and every failure is a lesson that brings us closer to success."
