About

Robotics Master's Student

I am currently a second-year master's student specializing in Robotics at the University at Buffalo, having completed my undergraduate degree in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Design from Shiv Nadar University. My academic journey has equipped me with a strong foundation in engineering principles, complemented by a creative flair fostered through my design minor.
My research interests are deeply rooted in the intricate world of control systems, perception, Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), motion planning, and manipulation. I am particularly fascinated by the way these areas interplay to bring about sophisticated robotic systems capable of interacting intelligently with their environment. I am committed to contributing to advancements in these fields, aiming to develop innovative solutions that can address real-world challenges.
Throughout my academic career, I have actively sought opportunities to expand my knowledge and skills in these areas. I have engaged in various projects and research endeavors that have allowed me to apply theoretical knowledge to practical scenarios, further fueling my passion for robotics. My goal is to leverage my expertise and experiences to drive the field of robotics toward new horizons, ultimately improving the harmony between humans and machines in various applications.

Current research activities include:
1. Soft object manipulation for drug extraction
2. Imitation learning for robotic manipulation
3. Object detection, tracking and following on a four legged robot
